How big is Theodore Roosevelt National Park?
The park's total acreage is 70,466.89 acres.
When is the park open? Does the park ever close?
The park is open 24 hours a day, year-round.
Are pets allowed?
Yes, with restrictions. Be aware that pets, especially dogs, can upset park wildlife and create a dangerous situation.
Where can I hike?
There are nearly 100 miles of trails in Theodore Roosevelt National Park. Hiking trails are shown on the Park Map.
Can I camp in the backcountry?
Yes.
Can I canoe or kayak the Little Missouri River through the park?
Yes. The Little Missouri River is typically quite shallow, and May and June are the best months to run the river.
Where can I ride my bicycle?
Park roads are open to bicycles. Off-road bicycling, including on hiking trails, is prohibited.
Can I bring my horse to the park?
Yes, the park is open for private horse use.
Where can I camp?
The Cottonwood Campground in the South Unit and the Juniper Campground in the North Unit are open year-round for camping.
Are bison dangerous?
Bison may be generally passive, but they can become agitated and charge without warning.
